%section% Negation

Negation is a controversial topic because Tolkien kept changing his mind on how it worked.

There are two chief forms of negation in Sindarin: **u-negation** and **la-negation**.

There's also an interjection **baw** and a verb **pen-** to express lack of something.

Brief history

U-negation began in early Gnomish, then it reappeared in Early Noldorin and became the Sindarin negative prefix **ú-** in the 1930s–50s, sometimes with an added "bad/unpleasant" sense.

La-negation originated from primitive syllabic [ḷ] and appeared early as prefixes like il- or al- meaning "no/not" or "opposite", sometimes shifting form before labials.

It largely disappeared in Early Noldorin, then re-emerged in the 1930s as al- "no, not", which survived in alfirin "immortal".

In 1969, Tolkien briefly rejected *u-negation*, restoring *la-negation* for Quenya, and reinterpreting **ú-** as "bad/difficult/impossible".

Tolkien never provided any Sindarin examples of verbal negation with *la-negation*, leaving its function mainly as a negative prefix of variable strength.

Although not his last idea of a negation paradigm, *la-negation* is well-attested enough to be acceptable to be used along with *u-negation*. So the recommendation is to use both.

U-negation

The most complete system was described in 1959, where **ú-** served as the general verbal negator, inducing §soft mutation§, forming quasi-participles, creating adjectives of impossibility (with **-ui**), and underlying an archaic negative verb (**ui** / **ûn**) that could act as a negative copula.

Under that system, **úchebin** would mean "I cannot keep" rather than "I do not keep". But a later note reverses this again: "*Back to ú ... ū should be negative particle*", effectively restoring *u-negation*.

Therefore this is recommended for Neo-Sindarin, especially when negating verbs.

The *u-negation* produces several distinct negative morphemes with different use cases. For example:

  • A verbal negator **ú-** which (probably) works as a clitic.
    E.g. *ú-chebin* "I do not keep".
  • A negative prefix **ú-**, which when attached to adjectives/participles simply negates or reverses their meaning.
    E.g. *nodui* "countable" × *únodui* "countless, uncountable".
  • When attached to a noun it creates a noun or an adjective with a related negative meaning.
    E.g. *carth* "deed" × *úgarth* "trespass, misdeed"; *talraph* n. "stirrup" × *udalraph* adj. "stirrupless".
  • There might also be a negative particle **ú** to negate the existence of a noun.
How to use u-negation

I recommend using *u-negation* with verbs.

Simply prefix the verb with *ú-*, and then apply §soft mutation§ to it: 

   beria- = "to protect"
  ú§veria-§ = "to not protect"

   panna- = "to arrange"
  ú§banna-§ = "to not arrange"

La-negation

Similarly to *u-negation*, the *la-negation* also produces distinct negative morphemes.

The *la-negation* mostly operates via the **al-** prefix, but it also manifests itself as a negative particle **law**.

Like *u-negation*, it can be used as a clitic verbal negator **ló** before consonants (*ló-chebin*) and **law** before vowels (*law-aphon*).

How to use la-negation

Just like *u-negation*, simply prefix a word, particularly a noun or adjective, with *al-*, and then apply §soft mutation§ to the word: 

    baradh = "steep"
  al§varadh§ = "not steep"

    nûr    = "deep"
  alnûr    = "shallow"

The negative particle [law](https://eldamo.org/content/words/word-393893289.html) can be used as an adverb, typically before the negated word: 

  **law** ill hain idh·§§reniar§§ líthennin = "**not** all those who wander [are] lost"

Negation of volition

While the **u-negation** and **la-negation** both are concerned with negation of facts, there is a different way to express negation of volition, known as "baw-negation," due to its origin from the root [√BĀ/ABA](https://eldamo.org/content/words/word-1104639711.html).

This root actually yields two forms of negation.

The first form is **baw**, derived from the verb *boda-*, meaning "to ban, prohibit", is an interjection used to emphatically express refusal, but it can also be used as a quick interjection "don't!".

The second form is **avo**, which comes from *avad*, meaning "refusal, reluctance". **Avo** is simply used to express a negative command. Its prefixed form is **av-**, but it can also be suffixed with pronominal endings: *avon* "I won't", *avof* "we won't".

Commands addressed to others use **avo**, followed by the regular imperative verb to express command: *avo §mado§vado§!* "don't eat!"

How to use baw-negation

For refusing things, use **baw** as interjection. For prohibiting someone, use **avo** plus imperative: 

  Lindir:  Gliro e·§glaer§'laer§ ad!         = "Recite the poem again!"
  Legolas: Baw! Si §boe§mboe§ annin lored. = "No! I must sleep now."

  Elendil: Iuithathon e·§gwachaedir§'wachaedir§ ad... = "I will use the palantír again..."
  Elrond:  Baw! Avo apho e·§gwachaedir§'wachaedir§!   = "Don't! Do not touch the palantír!"

Note that **avo** can be inflected as a regular verb: 

  avon mened = "I won't go"
  avo saedad = "he won't teach"
  avof       = "we won't"

Negative concord

Negative concord describes systems where multiple negative elements in a clause express a single semantic negation rather than cancelling each other out.

For example, in French *ils **n'ont** compris **aucun** mot* "they **didn't** understand **any** word", both *ne* and *aucun* contribute to one negation.

Sindarin seem to lack this kind of mechanic, so to express the same idea, we can use single negation, like in German: *Sie verstanden **kein** Wort* "they understood **no** word": 

  henianner **ú-beth** o lam i·Naugrim = "‹they understood› **no word** from [the] language [of] the Naugrim"

Recommendation

For Neo-Sindarin verbs, it's best to use the attested *u-negation* (**ú-** with §soft mutation§), since it is well described and appears to have been restored by Tolkien after 1969, whereas *la-negation* has no Sindarin verbal examples and relies partly on neologisms (e.g., law "no, not").

For all other negation, *la-negation* is still retained as a neutral negative prefix (especially al-).

For negatives of volition, Neo-Sindarin should follow Tolkien's attested system of using *baw* and *avo*.

The best-known Sindarin example is ***úchebin*** *estel anim* "I have kept no hope for myself".

Here, **ú-** is a neutral verbal negator. Similar prefixes appear in later vocabulary (*udalraph*, *úmarth*, *úgarth*).
