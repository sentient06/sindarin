Medial development

What "medial development" means

Medial development refers to sound changes affecting consonants (and clusters) in the middle of a word as it evolves from an earlier stage to a later one; or, what happens to consonants when something is added after them, as for example when suffixes cause consonants to become medial, so Sindarin causes some regular internal sound changes.

In Eldarin, this mainly concerns:

  • Stop → spirant weakening
  • Nasal + stop assimilation
  • Gemination and simplification
  • Lenition-like voicing and softening

This is a standard historical-linguistic phenomenon, not Tolkien-specific.

Across real languages, word-medial consonants are the most unstable. They weaken more easily than initials, they assimilate to neighbours, and they simplify in clusters.

Examples from real languages:

  • Latin vita → Spanish vida (t → d between vowels)
  • Latin lupus → Spanish lobo (p → b between vowels)
  • Old English habban → Modern have (bb → v)

This is called intervocalic lenition and cluster assimilation. Tolkien deliberately modelled Eldarin sound laws on these principles.

Core types of medial development in Sindarin

The main mechanisms seemt o be the following:

Intervocalic lenition (between vowels)

When a consonant sits between vowels, it often weakens (lenites). 

  Primitive   Sindarin outcome   Example
  -p-         -b-                le**p**etā → le**b**ed
  -t-         -d-                ne**t**er  → ne**d**er
  -k-         -g-                ma**k**ār  → ma**g**or
  -b-         -v-                a**b**a-   → a**v**ad
  -d-         -dh-               e**d**elā  → e**dh**el
  -g-          -                 khō**g**o  → hû**[']**

Medial clusters simplify

When two consonants meet inside a word, Sindarin often simplifies them.

Stop + t clusters often become spirants: 

  Primitive   Sindarin outcome   Example
  -kt-        -th-               te**kt**a   → tei**th**a-
  -pt-        -ph-               thi**pt**ha → ti**ph**-
  -gt-        -th/dh-            ma**gt**ā   → mae**th**a-

When a stop follows a nasal, it becomes voiced. 

  Primitive   Sindarin outcome   Example
  -nt-        -nd-               a
  -mp-        -mb-               a
  -nk-        -ng-               a
