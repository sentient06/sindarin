Summary

A copula is a word that connects the subject to its predicate. Sindarin uses a "zero-copula" in the present tense.

Future tense and past tense are not attested, so we use copulas which are neologisms. 

  Present tense:
  Elrond ⏝ ennas   = "Elrond [is] there"

  Past tense:
  Elrond nî ennas  = "Elrond was there"

  Future tense:
  Elrond tho ennas = "Elrond will be there"

  Alternate future tense:
  Elrond natho ennas = "Elrond will be there"
