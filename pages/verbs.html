Summary

Sindarin verbs have three indicative tenses: past, present and future, and are inflected for tense, number and person.

There are three verb classes:

  • Basic (i-stem);
  • Derived (a-stem); and
  • Half-strong verbs.

The verb class determines how the verb is conjugated.

Verbs can also be used as verbal nouns, made into active participles, and passive participles.

There are a few irregular verbs, and some defective verbs. There is a group of impersonal verbs that use the third person.
