Summary

Prepositions in Sindarin are numerous, but not exhaustive. Many are neologisms.

They are lenited when used adverbially immediately after a verb, and they cause a number of mutations of their own, since a number of them absorb the article.

Prepositions are normally monosyllabic.

There are different prepositions that look very similar and have partially coalesced, but have very different meanings.
