Notes

  1. Liquid mutation works the same when triggered by /l/, except for replacing the trigger's [r] with [l].
  2. Historically *rs > ss*, *ls > lθ*, with the latter further developing into *ll*, but likely not across a clitic/prefix boundary. As such we expect assimilated forms in liquid mutation, e.g.
    *or + sarn > os sarn, adel + sarn > adel tharn*
  3. In late Sindarin, *lh*, *rh*, and *wh* resist all mutations.
  4. In late Sindarin lenition of /m/ may be optional or altogether absent.
  5. Initial *gw-* is /w/ in conservative Sindarin, with mutations as outlined. In late Sindarin it is commonly reanalysed as /gw/. See second row.
  6. A single attested word constitutes an exception in conservative/archaic Sindarin: *rhass* < ✶krasse. Later analogical to normal *rh-*.
